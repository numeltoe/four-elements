The Aurora Australis, or Southern Lights

Auroras are caused by the solar wind--streams of tiny particles speeding toward Earth from the Sun--interacting with Earth's magnetosphere, or shield of magnetic field. The auroras are usually found overhead the polar regions.

Noctilucent clouds

Noctilucent clouds are formed by tiny ice crystals, and can be found between 47 and 53 miles above the ground, in the mesosphere. Their name means "night shining" in Latin, which is quite appropriate, as they are only visible during twilight.

The layers of Earth's atmosphere

The atmosphere of Earth is roughly 78% nitrogen, and 21% oxygen. Most of this oxygen, which we require to live, was produced by photosynthetic organisms like plants and cyanobacteria. Thanks, guys!

A rufous hummingbird, Selasphorus rufus

A rufous hummingbird in flight. Despite weighing about the same as a penny, these small birds fly 2,000 miles during their migration from the Rocky Mountains to the Mexican state of Guerrero.

High elevation white pine tree showing the Krummholz effect

Krummholz, or "bent wood" in German, is a type of stunted and twisted vegetation found in subalpine environments, formed by exposure to fierce winds. Although air itself is not visible to the naked eye, it shows itself to us in ways like this nevertheless.

Widow skimmer dragonfly, Libellula luctuosa

The widow skimmer is found throughout the Unite States, and in southeastern Canada. Dragonflies existed 325 million years ago; these dragnoflies had wingspans of nearly three feet. How'd you like to see one of those land next to your picnic basket?

A rubber cast of human lungs.

Together, the lungs contain about 1,500 miles of airways. Our breathing is controlled by the autonomic nervous system, which is why we don't have to think about it--which is pretty great, when you think about it!
